We are building a single-binary, all-Rust geocoder service (no Python at runtime, no Python ML deploy story).

The component in question is a small byte-level transformer: 2-4 million parameters, max sequence length ~64 bytes (input is a raw UTF-8 address string, byte-tokenized), with two output heads:

1. BIO sequence-tagging head over byte positions (street / house-number / postcode / locality / unit / etc.)
2. A country posterior head that produces a softmax over O(200) country IDs.

Constraints:

- The whole geocoder service ships as one statically-linked Rust binary (no Python child process, no LibTorch DLL surprise on a customer box). Inference must be pure Rust, or pure-Rust + a small native library we can vendor.
- Inference latency target: p99 well under 50 microseconds for one address on one CPU thread (we will sometimes batch, but the single-query path matters because admission control sometimes serializes queries).
- Throughput target on the surrounding system: 50,000 to 100,000 addresses per second per machine.
- Production model file should be small — target around 8 MB. We expect to apply post-training quantization (int8 or 4-bit) and we want the framework to actually have a quantization story we can rely on, not a "you can write your own" gesture.
- The training pipeline runs offline, not in production. Training ergonomics matter, but they are second priority. We need: BIO tagging supervision, gradient accumulation (small GPU at first), a multi-task loss combining the two heads, and ideally mixed-precision training.
- The license of the surrounding workspace is AGPL-3.0-or-later. Whatever framework we depend on must be MIT or Apache-2.0 (or BSD, or similarly permissive). LGPL or strong copyleft frameworks are problematic.
- Maintenance velocity matters — we need a framework that is alive in 2026, fixing bugs, accepting patches, releasing.
- Bonus, not required: WASM target, embedded deploy story.

The two pure-Rust ML frameworks we are weighing:

A. candle (huggingface/candle) — minimalist, HuggingFace-backed, leverages safetensors and HuggingFace ecosystem.
B. burn (tracel-ai/burn) — independent project, generic over backends (ndarray, candle backend, wgpu, libtorch backend).

Please give a blind technical recommendation. Cover:

- Inference latency for our target scale (small transformer on CPU): which framework will be faster, why, and what it depends on (BLAS vendor, intrinsics, autovec).
- Quantization story: which framework today actually supports int8 or 4-bit inference for a custom transformer? What are the gotchas?
- Training ergonomics for our specific shape (multi-head, BIO loss, gradient accumulation, mixed precision): which is less painful?
- Maintenance velocity over the past 6 months. Release cadence, issue responsiveness, governance, single points of failure.
- License: confirm both are permissive (MIT / Apache-2.0) and compatible with an AGPL-3.0-or-later workspace.
- Built-in transformer building blocks: does either ship a layer we can subclass, or do we hand-roll multi-head attention and the encoder block? Which path is less risky.
- Any hidden gotchas you know about either project — e.g. compile times, target architecture quirks, PyTorch-state-dict compatibility, ONNX export pain, missing ops.

Do not assume the answer. Where you do not know, say so. Where the two frameworks are roughly equivalent, say so. Where one has a clear edge for our specific workload (very small transformer, byte-level input, int8 quantization, CPU inference, AGPL workspace, single-binary deploy), call it.

If you want to suggest a third option (tract, ort/ONNX Runtime, tch-rs, LibTorch via tch, custom code), say so explicitly and explain the cost of going off the candle/burn path.
